# Electrician's Cheat Sheet

## **Essential Formulas**

### 1. **Ohm's Law**

\[
V = I \times R
\]

- **V** = Voltage (Volts)
- **I** = Current (Amps)
- **R** = Resistance (Ohms)

### 2. **Power Calculation**

\[
P = V \times I
\]

- **P** = Power (Watts)
- **V** = Voltage (Volts)
- **I** = Current (Amps)

### 3. **Current for a Given Load**

\[
I = \frac{P}{V}
\]

- **I** = Current (Amps)
- **P** = Power (Watts)
- **V** = Voltage (Volts)

### 4. **Resistance of a Wire**

\[
R = \rho \times \frac{L}{A}
\]

- **R** = Resistance (Ohms)
- **ρ** = Resistivity of the wire material (Ohm-meters)
- **L** = Length of the wire (meters)
- **A** = Cross-sectional area of the wire (m²)

### 5. **Voltage Drop in Wires**

\[
V_{drop} = I \times R
\]

- **V_drop** = Voltage drop (Volts)
- **I** = Current (Amps)
- **R** = Resistance (Ohms)

### 6. **Conductor Sizing Based on Current**

- For **2.5mm² wire** for general use (e.g., lighting, outlets):
    - Max. load = 20A for short runs, up to 16A for longer runs.
    - **Wire gauge calculation** should consider distance and current load.

## **How to Get Help**

### 1. **Ask Colleagues or Experienced Electricians**

- Sometimes the quickest way to solve a problem is to ask someone with more experience.
- Utilize **workshops, seminars, and trade conferences** to connect with others in the field.

### 2. **Use Online Resources**

- **Forums and Online Communities** (e.g., Reddit’s electrical communities or dedicated electrician forums).
- **YouTube Channels**: Many professional electricians share tutorials on installation techniques, safety practices, and
  troubleshooting.
- **Online Calculators**: Use websites to calculate wire sizes, voltage drops, and circuit requirements.

### 3. **Manufacturer Support**

- **Manuals & Technical Support**: For specific tools, appliances, or systems, always refer to the **manufacturer’s
  manuals** or **customer support**.
- **Supplier Contact**: Contact your materials supplier for advice on products, installation requirements, and safety
  standards.

### 4. **Call for Professional Help**

- **Licensed Electricians**: For complex systems or when uncertain, hire a licensed professional.
- **Utility Companies**: If you encounter issues with main supply lines or need disconnection, always contact your local
  **utility provider**.

### 5. **Consult Safety Guidelines**

- Refer to **OSHA** (Occupational Safety and Health Administration) or your country's equivalent for safety standards.
- **Electrical Codes**: Always adhere to national or local codes, such as **NEC (National Electrical Code)**, **IEC (
  International Electrotechnical Commission)**, or **BS7671** (UK).

## **Quick Troubleshooting Guide**

### Common Electrical Issues:

- **No Power to Outlet**: Check for tripped circuit breakers, loose connections, or faulty wiring.
- **Flickering Lights**: This could indicate loose wiring, faulty bulbs, or overloaded circuits.
- **Overloaded Circuit**: If the breaker trips, unplug devices and reset the breaker. If the breaker trips again, you
  may need to install a new circuit.
- **Hot Outlets**: This could be a sign of overload or damaged wiring. Turn off the circuit and inspect for loose
  connections or faulty wiring.

## **Best Practices for Cost and Time Efficiency**

1. **Bulk Buying**:
    - Purchase commonly used materials (wires, outlets, screws, etc.) in bulk to get discounts.
    - For projects with frequent work, buy tools and materials in advance to avoid last-minute purchases.

2. **Efficient Tool Use**:
    - Organize your tools in a **tool belt** or a **toolbox** for easy access.
    - Ensure all tools are in working condition before starting the job to avoid delays.

3. **Plan Your Work in Phases**:
    - Plan out electrical work in manageable phases to minimize wasted time.
    - Prioritize critical systems first, such as lighting and outlets, before moving on to appliance connections or
      specialized circuits.

4. **Safety and Precaution**:
    - **Always double-check circuit breakers** before working on any live circuits.
    - Use **insulated tools** and wear **PPE** (Personal Protective Equipment) for safety.

5. **Preventive Maintenance**:
    - Regularly inspect electrical installations for signs of wear or potential issues.
    - Keep wiring and connections clean and well-maintained to extend the lifespan of the system.
