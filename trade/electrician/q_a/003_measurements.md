# Measuring Units in the Electrical World (Ordered from Smaller to Larger)

| **Unit**                    | **Symbol** | **Description**                                                                                                          |
|-----------------------------|------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Charge**                  | C          | The quantity of electricity. Measured in coulombs (C).                                                                   |
| **Current**                 | A          | The flow of electric charge through a conductor. Measured in amperes (A).                                                |
| **Voltage**                 | V          | The electric potential difference between two points. Measured in volts (V).                                             |
| **Resistance**              | Ω          | The opposition to the flow of current in a circuit. Measured in ohms (Ω).                                                |
| **Power**                   | W          | The rate at which electrical energy is consumed or produced. Measured in watts (W).                                      |
| **Energy**                  | Wh, kWh, J | The capacity to do work. Measured in watt-hours (Wh), kilowatt-hours (kWh), or joules (J).                               |
| **Frequency**               | Hz         | The number of cycles per second of an alternating current (AC) signal. Measured in hertz (Hz).                           |
| **Capacitance**             | F          | The ability of a system to store charge. Measured in farads (F).                                                         |
| **Inductance**              | H          | The ability of a conductor to induce a voltage due to a changing current. Measured in henrys (H).                        |
| **Impedance**               | Z          | The total opposition to current in an AC circuit, combining resistance and reactance. Measured in ohms (Ω).              |
| **Reactance**               | X          | The opposition to the flow of alternating current due to capacitors and inductors. Measured in ohms (Ω).                 |
| **Conductance**             | S          | The reciprocal of resistance. Measured in siemens (S).                                                                   |
| **Magnetic Field Strength** | A/m        | The intensity of the magnetic field. Measured in amperes per meter (A/m).                                                |
| **Electric Field Strength** | V/m        | The force exerted on a unit charge in an electric field. Measured in volts per meter (V/m).                              |
| **Magnetic Flux Density**   | T          | The amount of magnetic flux passing through a unit area. Measured in teslas (T).                                         |
| **Magnetic Flux**           | Wb         | The amount of magnetism, taking into account the strength and the extent of the magnetic field. Measured in webers (Wb). |
| **Temperature**             | °C, K      | The degree of heat. Measured in degrees Celsius (°C) or kelvins (K).                                                     |
| **Power Factor**            | PF         | The ratio of real power to apparent power in a circuit. Measured as a dimensionless number between 0 and 1.              |
| **Voltage Drop**            | V          | The reduction in voltage in the electrical circuit between the source and the load. Measured in volts (V).               |
| **Electrical Efficiency**   | %          | The ratio of useful power output to total power input, expressed as a percentage. Measured in percentage (%).            |
| **Energy Consumption**      | kWh        | The amount of electrical energy consumed over time. Measured in kilowatt-hours (kWh).                                    |
| **Surge Protection**        |            | Protects electrical devices from sudden spikes in voltage, measured in joules (J) or other related units.                |
| **Circuit Capacity**        |            | Maximum current a circuit can safely carry, typically measured in amperes (A).                                           |
